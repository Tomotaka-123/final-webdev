Course Title

Class: Cyberethics

Semester: Fall 2021

Description: This course explores ethical and legal issues that arise in the use of technology and the internet. Topics covered include internet governance, regulating and governing technology, intellectual property, privacy, free speech, and censorship in cyberspace, among others. Students will learn to analyze and evaluate these issues through case studies and biblical contexts. The course also examines the impact of technology on society, particularly in third world countries, and the ethical considerations that arise. Students will develop critical thinking skills and apply ethical principles to real-world situations. By the end of the course, students will have a better understanding of cyberethics and the responsibilities of individuals and organizations in the use and development of technology.
