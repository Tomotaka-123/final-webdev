Course Title

Class: Networking

Semester: Spring 2022

Description: This course is an introductory-level course in computer networking that covers the fundamental concepts of computer networks and their applications. The course provides students with a comprehensive understanding of network protocols, architectures, and technologies, including the OSI and TCP/IP models, LANs, WANs, network security, wireless networks, and network troubleshooting. Throughout the course, students will engage in hands-on exercises using the Cisco Packet Tracer simulation software to practice configuring network devices and to troubleshoot network connectivity issues. The course also includes lab tasks, current event article discussions, and exams to assess students' understanding of the course materials. By the end of the course, students will have gained practical knowledge and skills in computer networking, which will enable them to pursue further studies in networking or enter the workforce as network administrators, IT support professionals, or other related roles.

  • TCP/IP
  • OSI model
  • Subnet mask
  • Packet Tracer

Skills Learned:

  • Virtual Environment
  • Subnetting
  • Cisco Switch/Router
  • VLAN
